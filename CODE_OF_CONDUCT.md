# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers of **PIIK.ME** pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Community Values

We value:

- Respectful communication
- Constructive collaboration
- Openness to learning and feedback
- Inclusivity and accessibility
- A welcoming environment for contributors of all experience levels

---

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the project
- Showing empathy and kindness toward other community members
- Acknowledging mistakes, learning from them, and moving forward

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- The use of sexualized language or imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention or advances
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- Public or private harassment of any kind
- Publishing others' private information (such as a physical or email address) without explicit permission
- Deliberate intimidation, stalking, or following
- Sustained disruption of community discussions or contributions
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## Good Collaboration Practices

To help maintain a productive and welcoming community:

- Search existing issues before opening a new one
- Keep discussions focused on the relevant topic
- Assume good intent when interacting with others
- Provide constructive feedback and suggestions
- Help newcomers learn and contribute effectively
- Respect differing technical opinions and approaches

---

## Code Review Etiquette

When reviewing contributions:

- Focus feedback on the contribution, not the contributor
- Explain requested changes clearly and respectfully
- Be open to alternative solutions and viewpoints
- Avoid dismissive, hostile, or sarcastic language
- Recognize and appreciate contributor effort

---

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior. They will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

---

## Maintainer Accountability

Maintainers are expected to uphold and model the standards described in this Code of Conduct.

Reports involving maintainers will be handled with the same seriousness, fairness, and confidentiality as reports involving any other community member.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions, the repository itself, and any other community forums operated by PIIK.ME. It also applies when an individual is officially representing the project in public spaces (for example, posting from an official account or representing PIIK.ME at an event).

---

## Reporting

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported by opening a confidential discussion in GitHub Discussions or by contacting a project maintainer through the contact information available on their GitHub profile.

All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The privacy and security of reporters will be respected to the fullest extent possible.

---

## Enforcement Guidelines

Maintainers will follow these guidelines when determining consequences for violations:

### 1. Correction

**Impact:** Minor, first-time violation.

A private written warning explaining the nature of the violation and why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Impact:** A single incident or series of actions.

A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved for a specified period, including unsolicited interaction in community spaces. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban

**Impact:** Serious or sustained violation.

A temporary ban from any kind of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period. No public or private interaction with the people involved is allowed during this period.

### 4. Permanent Ban

**Impact:** A pattern of violations, harassment, or aggression.

A permanent ban from any kind of public interaction within the community.

---

## Appeals

If an individual believes an enforcement action was made in error, they may request a review by contacting the maintainers through the available project communication channels.

Appeals will be reviewed fairly and decisions will be communicated whenever appropriate.
